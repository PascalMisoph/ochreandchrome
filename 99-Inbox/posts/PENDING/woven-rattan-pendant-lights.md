# The Woven Rattan Pendant Edit: 7 Fixtures for a Warm, Layered Room

Most rooms fail at the ceiling. The floor gets a rug, the sofa gets a throw, the shelves get their ceramics — and then the eye travels up to a flat builder-grade dome light that undoes all of it.

A woven rattan pendant fixes that in one move. It is the cheapest way to put texture overhead, where almost nothing else in the room can reach. The handwoven shade does two jobs at once: it reads as craft from across the room, and it filters the bulb into a warm, dappled pool instead of a flat wash.

The catch is that "rattan pendant" now covers everything from genuinely beautiful woven shades to thin, gappy baskets that sag within a year. Below are seven we would actually hang — each chosen for the weave, the scale, and the quality of light it throws, with the trade-offs named honestly.

## What Makes a Good Woven Rattan Pendant

- **Weave density.** A tight, even weave casts a controlled shadow pattern and holds its shape. Loose, open baskets throw harsh gaps of light and distort over time.
- **Scale.** A pendant should be roughly half to two-thirds the width of the table or island beneath it. Undersized rattan reads as an afterthought; oversized needs ceiling height to breathe.
- **Finish honesty.** Natural, coffee, and black colorways belong in a [layered, grounded room](/guides/afrohemian-rooms-with-soul/). Avoid orange-toned lacquered rattan — it photographs cheap and fights warm neutrals.
- **Bulb temperature.** Rattan rewards a warm 2700K bulb. Cool white turns the whole point of the fixture cold and clinical.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How high should I hang a woven rattan pendant?** Over a table or island, leave 30–36 inches between the bottom of the shade and the surface. As a room centerpiece, keep the bottom at least 7 feet off the floor so you are not walking into it.

**What bulb works best in a rattan pendant?** A warm 2700K LED. Rattan is built to be seen with warm light glowing through the weave — cool white undoes the texture and makes the fixture look flat.

**Are woven rattan pendants only for boho rooms?** No. Read as natural fiber rather than a style cliché, a woven pendant grounds modern, Afrohemian, and warm-minimalist rooms alike. The material does the work; keep the rest of the room restrained and it reads as craft, not costume.

**Do rattan shades hold up over time?** A tightly woven shade keeps its shape for years. The loose, gappy baskets are the ones that sag and discolor — which is why weave density leads our buying criteria.
