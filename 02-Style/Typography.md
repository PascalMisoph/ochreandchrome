# Typography

| Role | Font | Why it fits | Use for | Do NOT use for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Headline** | Playfair Display | Classic, editorial, high-end | H1s, Pin main titles | Body paragraphs, small UI |
| **Subheadline** | Lato (Light or Regular) | Clean, modern, highly legible | H2s, H3s, pin sub-titles | Massive hero text |
| **Body** | Lato (Regular) | Perfect mobile readability | All long-form blog text | Over images without a background plate |
| **Small label** | Montserrat (All Caps, wide tracking) | Premium fashion/decor aesthetic | Category tags, "Shop the Look" badges | Sentences > 5 words |

## Pairings

- **Pinterest pin overlay:** Playfair Display **Bold** for the hook word + Lato Regular for the rest of the sentence.
- **Website:** Playfair Display (headers) + Lato (body).

## Hard rules

- Never cursive or script fonts.
- **Sublines and subheadings are never italic.** Always set Lato (or any clean sans) upright. Italic on sublines reads soft and "lifestyle blog"; upright reads editorial and premium.
- **Sublines and subheadings never end with a period.** They are typographic labels, not sentences. A question mark or em-dash is fine when grammatically required; a full stop is not. (Headlines may carry a period for editorial effect; sublines may not.)
- Never put body copy directly over a busy image — always use a Cream backing plate or gradient.
- Maintain high contrast (Navy on Cream).
