# Adire Textile — Visual Guide

A specialist sub-guide for Adire-inspired and authentic Adire pin content. **Read [[Cultural-Care-Guidelines]] before producing anything in this category.**

Adire is a Yoruba indigo resist-dye textile tradition from Nigeria — primarily Abeokuta and Ibadan. The pattern language is irregular, hand-drawn, and steeped in regional symbolism. The aesthetic has spread; the heritage is specific.

## What "Adire-style" looks like

- **Indigo + white** (occasionally indigo + cream when over-dyed cotton).
- **Resist-dye irregularity** — the white pattern is hand-blocked, hand-drawn with cassava paste, or stitched-and-tied. Never digitally perfect.
- **Pattern families** —
  - *Eleko* (cassava-paste hand-painted motifs)
  - *Alabere* (stitch-resist)
  - *Oniko* (tie-resist)
  - These are the documented categories. Use the names only when accurate.
- **Repeat scale** — generally medium (a single motif fits in a 4–8 inch tile). Avoid micro-prints, which read as commercial fabric.
- **Edge bleed** — natural indigo dye bleeds slightly. The edge is never crisp.

## Composition rules for pins

| Pin type | Layout |
|---|---|
| Close-up textile detail | 80% textile, 20% cream backing for overlay copy. Crop tight enough to see weave. |
| Styled-on-sofa | Linen sofa in cream/oat, 1–3 indigo cushions clustered, warm side-light |
| Framed wall art | A length of Adire-inspired textile, cream mat, dark walnut frame, hung against ivory or clay-plaster wall |
| Layered bed styling | Cream linen base, indigo throw at foot, terracotta lumbar, brass sconce above |
| Flat-lay moodboard | Folded indigo textile + brass candlestick + ceramic vessel + dried botanical, on warm cream |

## Caption discipline

- Documented Yoruba/Nigerian product → "**Adire**" (no qualifier).
- Adire-aesthetic textile of unknown origin → "**Adire-inspired indigo textile.**"
- AI-generated visual → never claim authenticity. "*Image: an indigo resist-dye textile aesthetic.*"
- Roundup mixing both → segment them: *"Authentic"* and *"Inspired"* sections. See [[Cultural-Care-Guidelines]].

## Midjourney prompt formula

> Editorial close-up photography of an indigo resist-dye textile in the Adire-inspired tradition, hand-drawn irregular pattern, slight dye bleed at edges, layered onto a warm cream linen sofa or framed against an ivory wall, warm afternoon natural light, 2:3 aspect ratio, generous negative space for text overlay, premium magazine composition, no people, no claims of cultural authenticity.

## Sample on-pin overlay copy

- *"The Adire Pillow Edit."*
- *"Authentic Adire vs. Inspired."* *(only use when copy follows through with both tiers)*
- *"Indigo, Layered."*
- *"What Is Adire?"* *(educational pin; link to Adire blog post)*

## Forbidden

- "Tribal indigo print." Never.
- "Ancient Yoruba symbol of [X]" without a documented citation.
- Calling generic batik or Indonesian shibori "Adire."
- Pastel-tinted "Adire-look" — not on-brand.
