# Logo Reference

The Ochre & Chrome logo is a **circular editorial still-life** — not a wordmark on a flat field. This is the brand mood encoded as a single image and should be treated as a visual reference even when used at small sizes.

Asset on disk: `ochrome-and-chrome-profile.png` (project root).

## What the logo contains

- A **circular editorial frame** — the still-life is composed inside a perfect circle.
- A **ribbed glass vase** with elegant vertical fluting (Neo Deco material).
- **Dried botanical stems** rising from the vase (Afrohemian textural cue).
- A **brass / chrome candlestick form** beside it (the metal axis of the brand name).
- **Warm shadow and candlelight atmosphere** — deep ochre and walnut tones, low directional light.
- **White editorial serif typography** reading *"Ochre & Chrome"*, set quietly within the frame — never shouted.

## The mood it encodes

| Cue | What it signals |
|---|---|
| Circular crop | Editorial, considered, magazine-cover |
| Ribbed glass vase | Fluted-glass material language (Neo Deco) |
| Dried stems | Tactile, soulful, collected (Afrohemian) |
| Brass / chrome candlestick | The two metals of the brand name |
| Warm shadow / candlelight | Cinematic restraint, evening editorial |
| Serif type | Premium magazine, never sans-only |
| Deep ochre tonality | Warmth as the dominant brand temperature |

## Use rules

- **Profile image (Pinterest, Instagram, email avatar).** Logo as-is, on its native warm background. Do not put it on a white square — it loses depth.
- **Website header.** Quiet wordmark companion only — the full circular still-life is too rich for a top-bar position. Use the type alone in Playfair Display, set in Deep Navy `#1A2A3A` on Warm Cream `#F7F5F0`.
- **Pin watermark.** Bottom-centre, 12pt Montserrat domain (`ochreandchrome.com`) — not the logo image. The logo is too detailed for pin scale.
- **Email / lead magnet.** Full circular logo at the top of the cover and footer.
- **Co-brand / partnership.** Provide the logo on its native warm background; never re-key it onto white.

## Do not

- Crop the circle into a square or rectangle.
- Replace the typography with a different font.
- Recolour the still-life to cool greys or pastels.
- Place over busy photography without a cream backing plate.
- Use the still-life image alone (without the wordmark) — the brand name is part of the lockup.
- Re-render the still-life via AI for "cleaner" versions — preserve the original asset.

## When working frontend

The logo's mood — circular, warm, candlelit, materially specific — is a useful brief for the website hero. The hero should *feel like* the logo: dark, warm, image-led, editorial. See [[Frontend-Art-Direction]] and [[Homepage-Mockup-Review]].
