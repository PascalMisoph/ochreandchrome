# Neo Deco vs Afrohemian — The Two Worlds

Ochre & Chrome stands on **two equal aesthetic worlds**, not one. Neither is a "side trend." Every piece of content belongs to one of them or to the explicit fusion between them.

## The two worlds

| | **Neo Deco** | **Afrohemian** |
|---|---|---|
| One-line definition | Modern Art Deco for the post-minimalist home | Soulful, collected interiors with global craft |
| Mood | Glamour. Geometry. Polish. | Warmth. Texture. Story. |

## How they relate

The brand name encodes the relationship. **Ochre** = the warmth, earthiness, and craft of Afrohemian. **Chrome** = the polish, geometry, and metal of Neo Deco. Their tension is the brand.

A single room can hold both. A Neo Deco living room with a Tonga basket cluster on the wall is *on-brand*. An Afrohemian bedroom with a brass arched sconce above a carved wood bench is *on-brand*. What is **off-brand**: a room that reads as one of them executed shallowly (e.g. only beige textiles + dreamcatcher = "boho", not Afrohemian; only brass + black = "industrial", not Neo Deco).

## The fusion zone

Cross-over content is its own category — not a mid-point, but an *intentional intersection*. Hallmarks:

- A red marble surface (Neo Deco) with a Yoruba-inspired indigo runner (Afrohemian).
- A brass pendant (Neo Deco) over a linen-and-jute table styled with terracotta vessels (Afrohemian).
- A velvet sofa (Neo Deco) layered with Adire-inspired cushions (Afrohemian).

Use fusion content sparingly — roughly 1 in 5 pins. Most pins should be a clean expression of one world; the fusion is the *occasional* prestige beat.

## How to know which world a pin belongs to

Ask in order:
1. **What is the hero material?** Brass / chrome / marble / velvet / lacquer → Neo Deco. Jute / linen / clay / carved wood / textile → Afrohemian.
2. **What is the geometry?** Hard, repeating, fan-arch, chevron → Neo Deco. Irregular, hand-formed, woven → Afrohemian.
3. **What is the palette key?** Cool-cream + jewel + metal → Neo Deco. Warm ochre + clay + tobacco → Afrohemian.

If two answers go to one world, that's the world. If they split evenly, it's a fusion pin.
