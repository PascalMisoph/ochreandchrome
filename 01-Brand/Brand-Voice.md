# Brand Voice & Copy Rules

## Voice Personality

The discerning, highly educated interior designer friend who wants you to avoid buying cheap garbage.

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Tone | Authoritative, curated, warm, slightly opinionated |
| Reading level | Grade 8–9; sophisticated vocab, short punchy sentences |
| Emotional feel | Inspiring and relieving (takes the overwhelm out of decorating) |
| Authority | Expert curator. Not DIYers — design strategists. |

## Words to use often

`Curated` · `intentional` · `structural` · `tactile` · `heirloom` · `architectural` · `focal point` · `edit` · `grounded`

## Words to AVOID

`Cute` · `cheap` · `vibe` · `hack` · `bling` · `pop of color` · ~~`boho-chic`~~ → use **Afrohemian**

## Phrases to use

- "Invest in…"
- "Elevate the space…"
- "Ground the room with…"
- "Structural integrity."

## Phrases to AVOID

- "Run don't walk"
- "Obsessed with this"
- "Link in bio"
- "You guys"

## How to sound premium

Focus on the **materials**. Describe the *weight* of the brass, the *weave* of the sisal, the *veining* in the marble.

## How to sound trustworthy

Point out flaws. Example: *"This chair is stunning, but it requires a large room to breathe. Do not buy this for a studio apartment."*

## How to avoid sounding like AI

- Vary sentence lengths.
- Use fragments for emphasis.
- Add specific sensory details AI usually misses (e.g. *"The matte finish absorbs light rather than reflecting it"*).

## How to avoid sounding like a dropshipping account

Never focus on the discount or shipping speed. Focus entirely on aesthetic value and material quality.

## Copywriting Rules

### Pinterest pin titles
- Max **40 characters** before truncation.
- Front-load the exact keyword.
- Use odd numbers for listicles.

### Pinterest pin descriptions
- Natural sentence first.
- Weave in 3–4 long-tail keywords.
- End with a specific CTA to click.
- Max 2–3 targeted hashtags.

### Blog introductions — PAS framework
1. **Problem** — Your house feels flat.
2. **Agitation** — Fast furniture breaks and lacks soul.
3. **Solution** — Here is how to integrate Afrohemian textures intentionally.

Max 3 short paragraphs.

### Product descriptions
Format: **What it is. Why it works stylistically. Who it's for.**

### CTA buttons
Action-oriented. Not "Click Here." Use *"Shop the Brass Pendant on CB2"* or *"View Current Pricing."*

### Affiliate disclosures
Top of post, italicized:
> *We independently curate these items. If you purchase through our links, we may earn a commission.*

### Email opt-ins
Focus on the exact outcome. *"Get the 2026 Metal Mixing Cheat Sheet."*

### Shop-the-look copy
Be prescriptive: *"Anchor the room with [Item 1], layer with [Item 2], and illuminate with [Item 3]."*

### Comparison content
Objective and structural. Compare durability, light reflection, stylistic longevity.

### Mistake-based content
Empathetic but firm: *"Mistake #1: Floating your rug. The Fix: Anchor your front furniture legs."*

### Trend content
Analytical. Explain *why* the trend is happening (e.g. reaction against sterile minimalism).

**Do not cite Pinterest as the authority.** We are the editorial. Frame trends as cultural and design movements ("the return of warm metals," "the post-minimalist shift toward texture"), never as "Pinterest predicts" or "Pinterest says."

### Afrohemian and Adire copy
- Use **"African-inspired"**, **"artisan-made"**, **"textile-led"**, **"collected"**, **"global craft"**, **"Adire-inspired"** when accurate.
- Do **not** invent cultural origin claims. Do not write "ancient tribal pattern" or "spiritual symbol" unless the piece is documented.
- When discussing Adire specifically, frame it as a Yoruba/Nigerian indigo resist-dye textile tradition — only when the product or context warrants it.
- For AI-generated visuals, never claim authenticity. Inspired ≠ authentic.
