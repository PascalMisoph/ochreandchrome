### What worked exceptionally

- **Cultural-care execution.** The indigo cushion has hand-drawn Adire-inspired geometry with slight irregularity — exactly what was asked. No invented "tribal" symbols, no glyphs.
- **Materials are textbook Afrohemian.** Slubby cream linen, sculptural carved wood (Senufo-feeling) stool, hand-thrown terracotta vessel with dried palm leaves, oxidised brass candlestick — all five core materials present.
- **Warm afternoon side-rake light** absorbs into the clay-plaster wall and linen exactly as specified — not bright, not floodlit.
- **The terracotta lumbar pillow** is the right shade of warm rust, not too saturated, reads as silk-blend linen.
- **Footer banner** rendered with the brushed-brass divider — first time that detail came through cleanly.

### One element to watch

The **macramé/woven wall hanging on the left** wasn't in the prompt. It reads as artisan textile and sits within Afrohemian's vocabulary, but **macramé has a 1970s American-hippie history** more than an African one. It's borderline "boho" rather than Afrohemian. For future Afrohemian pins, if a wall textile is shown, prefer specifying:
- "a framed Adire-inspired textile in a dark walnut frame," or
- "a single woven Tonga-style basket cluster on the wall."

Either is more on-brand than open-weave macramé.

### Tweaks to bundle if regenerating v2

1. **Drop the trailing period** on the subline — already enforced in §7.A.
2. **Spec the wall-art element explicitly** to avoid macramé invention. Add: *"on the wall behind and left of the sofa, a single woven natural-fibre basket of medium scale OR a small framed Adire-inspired textile — no other wall art."*

## Use as reference

Future agents producing **Afrohemian inspiration / educational pins** should:
1. Copy the §7.A prompt above.
2. Replace `{CATEGORY_LABEL}`, `{HEADLINE}`, `{SUBLINE}`, and the Zone 4 image brief with the new pin's values.
3. **Keep the cultural-care directive block intact** — it's the single most important paragraph for Afrohemian and Adire pins.
4. If wall art is part of the scene, specify it explicitly to avoid macramé / dreamcatcher / mandala drift.
