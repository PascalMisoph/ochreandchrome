# Pinterest Best Practices — 2026

How the Ochre & Chrome operator should think about Pinterest. Applies to every pin, every board, every week.

## Mental model

Pinterest is **a visual search-and-planning engine**, not a social feed. Users are in three states:

1. **Inspiration** (broad keywords, saving for later) — top of funnel.
2. **Planning** (narrower keywords, comparing options) — mid funnel.
3. **Purchase intent** (long-tail keywords, shoppable pins, "buy" / "best") — bottom of funnel.

Every pin has to declare which state it serves. A "Neo Deco Moodboard" is inspiration. "How to Mix Brass and Chrome" is planning. "7 Brass Pendants Under $300" is purchase intent.

## Format rules

- **2:3 vertical, 1000 × 1500 px.** Standard.
- **Tall format (1000 × 2100)** acceptable for Carousel and How-To with stacked steps. Don't use for hero pins.
- **Square (1000 × 1000)** discouraged — Pinterest deprioritises.
- **Video pins (6–15 seconds, 1080 × 1920 vertical).** Use for trend forecasts, room reveals, before/after. Slow cinematic pans, no quick cuts, warm key light, captions baked in.
- **Idea Pins / Carousels** — use for "5 ways to layer textiles" or "5 mistakes" content. Page 1 hooks, page 5 closes with CTA.

## Title rules

- **40 characters or front-load the keyword.** Pinterest truncates.
- One concrete number when applicable.
- Power word allowed but not mandatory: *Stunning, Designer-Approved, Editor's Pick, Curated.*
- No clickbait: don't promise what the destination doesn't deliver.

## Description rules (max 500 chars, fill to ~300–400)

1. **Sentence 1**: natural-language hook with the primary keyword.
2. **Sentence 2**: weave in 3 long-tail variants of the keyword.
3. **Sentence 3**: a concrete CTA. *"Read the full sourcing guide on Ochre & Chrome."*
4. **Hashtags**: 2–3 maximum, end of description, on-brand only. (`#NeoDeco #Afrohemian #InteriorDesign`)

## Board rules

- **Title**: `[Adjective/Modifier] + [Niche Keyword]` (per [[Pinterest-Profile]]).
- **Description**: 300–500 chars, keyword-stacked, written for human and search.
- **Cover image**: a hero pin from the board, on-brand.
- **Section structure**: avoid sections until a board crosses 100 pins.
- **First-90-days rule**: don't create a new board until the existing 6 each have 30+ pins. See [[Existing-Board-Audit]] and [[Board-Strategy]].

## SEO meta-rules

- Fill **every available field**: title, description, alt text, destination URL, board.
- Alt text mirrors the description's first sentence; describes the image plainly for accessibility *and* SEO.
- Use Pinterest Trends and predicts.pinterest.com to validate keyword direction quarterly — but don't *cite* Pinterest in copy. See [[Brand-Voice]].

## Cadence

- **Target: 2–3 pins per day.** 75 pins per 30 days. See [[30-Day-Pinterest-Content-Plan]].
- **Schedule via Pinterest native or Tailwind.** Spread the day's pins across morning + late-evening drops.
- **Fresh content weekly** — Pinterest favours new pins over re-pins from the same domain. *Do not* hoard.
- **Refresh strategy**: every Friday, take the 5 best-performing pins from the last 90 days and produce 5 fresh variations (new image, new headline) targeting the same URL.

## Engagement signals Pinterest weighs

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Saves | Algorithm's #1 quality proxy |
| Outbound clicks | What we actually sell — see [[KPIs-Tracking]] |
| Pin CTR (clicks/impressions) | Goal: > 1.0% |
| Long clicks (user stays on destination) | Prevents the algorithm flagging clickbait |
| Close-up taps | Indicates intent |

## What kills a pin's reach

- Wrong aspect ratio.
- Tiny unreadable mobile text.
- Cluttered overlapping product collage.
- Title and image disagree (clickbait).
- No destination URL (or a 404).
- Posting 10 pins at once instead of staggered.
- Identical headline/description copy across multiple pins (duplicate content).
- Affiliate link directly on the pin (Pinterest deprioritises bare affiliate URLs — always route through the site).

## What helps a pin's reach

- Searchable title + description with consistent keywords.
- Recognisable brand pattern (cream background, Playfair headline) — repeat saves train the algorithm.
- Save-worthy hook ("save for your renovation," "save this list").
- Niche-but-specific keyword pairings ("Neo Deco entryway styling" outperforms "modern home").
- Linking to a destination that *actually answers* the pin's promise.

## Quarterly Pinterest health check

Every 90 days:
1. Audit the top-10 pins by outbound clicks. What patterns repeat?
2. Audit the bottom-10. Why are they dead?
3. Re-write 10 board descriptions with refreshed long-tail stacks.
4. Validate any new keywords against current Pinterest Trends data.
5. Update [[Pinterest-Best-Practices-2026]] if Pinterest's algorithm guidance shifts.
