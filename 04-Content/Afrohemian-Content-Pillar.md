# Content Pillar — Afrohemian

One of the two macro worlds. See [[Neo-Deco-vs-Afrohemian]] for the relationship, [[Afrohemian-Visual-Guide]] for visual rules, and **[[Cultural-Care-Guidelines]] (mandatory before drafting)**.

## Strategic role

Afrohemian is the **growth half** of the brand. Pinterest search demand for Afrohemian, "afro bohemian," "warm modern," "collected interior," and "soulful home" has been rising year over year and is the fastest-moving aesthetic in 2026. Equal strategic weight to Neo Deco; in some quarters it carries more.

Affiliate AOV is mid-range (textiles, baskets, ceramics) but conversion velocity is high — readers buy a $90 pillow more easily than a $2,400 sofa.

## Voice notes specific to Afrohemian

- Lead with **texture**: "the slub of hand-loomed linen," "the irregular dye-bleed at the edge of the indigo."
- Lead with **provenance** when documented: "hand-dyed in Abeokuta," "woven by Tonga artisans in Zimbabwe."
- Use approved language from [[Cultural-Care-Guidelines]]:
  - "African-inspired" ✅
  - "Artisan-made" ✅ (when documented)
  - "Adire-inspired" ✅ (when not documented Yoruba)
  - "Tribal," "ethnic," "boho-chic" ❌
- Distinguish **authentic** vs **inspired** in every shoppable post.

## Cross-pillar bridges

- **Afrohemian × Neo Deco fusion:** A brass pendant (Neo Deco) hanging over a linen-and-jute table styled with terracotta vessels (Afrohemian). Use as the prestige beat.
- **Afrohemian × Adire:** Adire is a *sub-pillar* of Afrohemian — every Adire pin is also an Afrohemian pin, but not every Afrohemian pin is Adire.

## What kills an Afrohemian pin

1. "Tribal" or "ethnic" copy.
2. Beige-on-beige minimalism marketed as "warm modern."
3. Unattributed cultural symbols.
4. Pastel boho rainbow palette.
5. "Boho-chic" terminology.
6. AI-generated images claimed as authentic artisan work.
