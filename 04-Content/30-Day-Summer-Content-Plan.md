# 30-Day Summer Content Plan — 11 Articles · 90 Pins

The Q3 scale-up. Continues the **article-driven model** (1 blog article → 8 pins, distinct angles, same destination URL) established by A1–A5, now at **3 pins/day for 30 days** and weighted hard toward Afrohemian for the summer season. Pin IDs continue the A-series as **A6–A16**.

> **Predecessors:** A1–A5 (rattan, neo-deco lighting, jute rugs, burl wood, vessels) ran 2/day, 2026-06-02 → 06-19. This plan picks up 2026-06-25. The original 75-pin `30-Day-Pinterest-Content-Plan.md` is superseded by the article-driven model and kept for reference only.

## §data — Why this slate (data-driven basis)

Topic selection and per-pin angles are driven by real 2026 search-demand and the artisan/natural-material summer movement — **not** invented. Internal rationale only; **public copy never says "Pinterest predicts"** (brand voice rule — reframe as a cultural/editorial movement, see [[Brand-Voice]]).

- **Afrohemian is the season, not a side trend.** "afrobohemian home decor" search demand **+220%**; the broader Afrohemian fusion (Afrocentric artistry + organic materials + earthy palette + pattern) is one of 2026's strongest design signals. → justifies the 70% weighting.
- **Adire has real pull.** "adire fabric" **+130%** → the long-deferred Adire cluster finally ships as a flagship article (A7), feeding the underfed *Authentic Adire Fabric & Textiles* board.
- **Summer = artisan/natural materials.** Raffia, cane, rattan, seagrass, woven baskets are the confirmed summer-2026 artisan-décor leads (raw, organic, tactile, "foundation not finishing touch"). → A6, A12.
- **Earth-tone palette locked for summer:** clay, sand, terracotta, warm beige, ecru, olive. → A8, A13.

## §2 — Layout mix rule (the "mal editorial, mal full" fix)

The A1–A5 wave was **100% full-bleed (§7.B)**. This plan deliberately mixes both house layouts so the feed reads like the launch set (which alternated). **Layout is assigned per angle**, so every article auto-yields ~4 editorial + ~4 full-bleed:

| Angle (per article) | Layout | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 · listicle hook | **full-bleed §7.B** | hero photo sells the edit |
| 2 · mistake | **editorial §7.A** | "Stop X" reads as a magazine statement |
| 3 · how-to | **full-bleed §7.B** | the styled result is the proof |
| 4 · comparison (X vs Y) | **editorial §7.A** | label + headline frames the contrast |
| 5 · inspiration / moodboard | **full-bleed §7.B** | pure atmosphere |
| 6 · trend report | **editorial §7.A** | trend-cover energy (top kicker + serif) |
| 7 · how-to-choose | **full-bleed §7.B** | single-subject hero |
| 8 · before/after / fix | **editorial §7.A** | statement headline |
| 9 · (flagship extra) | full-bleed §7.B | anchor atmosphere |

Editorial = §7.A 5-zone (cream top kicker + Playfair headline + subline, photo centre, cream footer wordmark) — exactly P01/P03. Full-bleed = §7.B (edge-to-edge photo, floating lower-third headline, slim domain strip) — exactly the A-series. Both prompts live in [[AI-Prompts]].
